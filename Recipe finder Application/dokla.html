Steamed Dhokla Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil (for greasing)
  • 2 cups water (for steaming)
  • For tempering:
    • 1 tbsp oil
    • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
    • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
    • 8-10 curry leaves
    • 1-2 green chilies, slit
    • 1 tbsp sesame seeds (optional)
    • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Batter: In a large bowl, combine rice flour, chickpea flour, yogurt, ginger paste, baking soda, sugar, and salt. Gradually add water to make a smooth batter, similar to pancake batter consistency.
  2. Prepare the Steamer: Grease a steaming tray or a round pan with oil. Fill a steamer or large pot with water, bring it to a boil, and place the tray or pan in it.
  3. Steam the Dhokla: Pour the batter into the greased pan and smooth it out. Cover with a lid and steam for 15-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean when inserted into the center.
  4. Prepare Tempering: Heat oil in a small pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies. Once the seeds splutter, add sesame seeds (if using) and cook for a minute.
  5. Tempering the Dhokla: Once the dhokla is steamed and set, pour the tempering mixture over it. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  6. Serve: Let the dhokla cool for a few minutes before cutting it into pieces. Serve with green chutney or tamarind chutney.

Tip: You can also add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) in the tempering for extra flavor.
